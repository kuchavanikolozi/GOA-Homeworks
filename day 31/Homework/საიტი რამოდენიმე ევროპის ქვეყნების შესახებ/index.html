Georgia

Georgia, located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, is known for its ancient culture, traditions, and hospitality. One of the most significant traditions is the "Supra" or Georgian feast, where the host offers a variety of delicious Georgian dishes and toasts to friendship, love, and life.

Georgian traditional music, polyphonic singing, and dance are recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage. Wine-making also holds a central place in Georgian culture, with the country being one of the oldest wine producers in the world.

Sweden

Sweden is known for its rich cultural heritage, serene landscapes, and strong connection to nature. One of the most cherished Swedish traditions is Midsummer (Midsommar), celebrated in June, where people gather to dance around the maypole, sing traditional songs, and enjoy local dishes such as herring and new potatoes.

Another important tradition is the "Lucia" celebration in December, which marks the start of the Christmas season. It involves a procession led by a girl dressed as Saint Lucia, wearing a crown of candles, symbolizing the return of light during the dark winter months.

Festival Date Region
Midsummer (Midsommar) June Nationwide
Walpurgis Night (Valborg) April 30 Nationwide
Lucia December 13 Nationwide

Norway

Norway is celebrated for its breathtaking fjords, Viking heritage, and deep-rooted traditions. One of the most significant celebrations is Constitution Day (Syttende Mai) on May 17, where Norwegians take to the streets in national costumes, parades, and wave the Norwegian flag to celebrate the country's independence.

Another important tradition is "Lefse," a type of soft flatbread, often made during the Christmas season. The Sami people, the indigenous group of Norway, also maintain traditions such as reindeer herding, and their unique music and art are recognized and celebrated across the country.

Festival Date Region
Constitution Day (Syttende Mai) May 17 Nationwide
Sami National Day February 6 Nationwide (Sami regions)
St. John's Eve (Jonsok) June 23 Western Norway

The Netherlands

The Netherlands is known for its tulip fields, windmills, and rich traditions. One of the most famous Dutch celebrations is King's Day (Koningsdag), celebrated on April 27, where the entire country dresses in orange and enjoys street parties, festivals, and flea markets to honor the Dutch monarchy.

Another notable tradition is Sinterklaas, celebrated in December. The arrival of Sinterklaas by boat and the giving of gifts to children is an integral part of the Dutch holiday season. The Netherlands is also famous for its cycling culture, with bicycles being a significant part of daily life.

Festival Date Region
King's Day (Koningsdag) April 27 Nationwide
Sinterklaas December 5 Nationwide
Flower Parade (Bloemencorso) April Various Regions
